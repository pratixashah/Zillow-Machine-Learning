We all know that real estate plays an integral role in the U.S. economy. Residential real estate helps provide housing for families, and it happens to be the most significant source of wealth and savings for many people. Also, the home is the most extensive and most expensive purchase a person makes in his or her lifetime. Real estate trends have changed over time, and many factors weigh-in like recession, pandemic, market boom, etc. Therefore, understanding the real estate market is compelling to get more empowered as a buyer or seller.


Objective


  1. To create visualizations using Tableau to understand the various trends in the data and uncover some meaningful insights.

  2. To build a price forecast model and determine the direction of future trends for informed decision-making.

We got the objective. What Next??




Data Collection


  • As a team interested in home prices and residential real estate trends, we collected data from Zillow and Realtor during the ETL project.

  • The Realtor website was scrapped, and the data points like the “Price,” “Sqft Area,” “No. of Bedrooms”, “No of Bathrooms,” and “Zipcode” for a particular region was collected as these can be the features to predict the house prices in a specific area.

  • However, the scraping took quite a long time, and there was a time constraint to extend to other regions.

  • The research data from the Zillow website was extracted and transformed as part of the ETL project.

  • The dataset extracted, categorizes the homes and provides the ZHVI (Zillow Home Value Index) values that is the measure of home value and market changes across a given region and housing types.

  • The other extracted data included monthly sales, inventory listings, and rentals data (market-rate rent across a given area).

  • The data collected from Zillow was a time series and had data from 1996, which was compelling to do a time series forecasting model.
